## 📌 Introduction

In modern organizations, cybersecurity is not just about prevention—it’s about **continuous monitoring and fast response**.

This is where the **Security Operations Center (SOC)** plays a critical role.

A SOC is the **central hub** where security analysts monitor systems, detect threats, and respond to cyberattacks in real time.

---

## 🧠 What is a SOC?

A **Security Operations Center (SOC)** is a centralized unit responsible for:

* Monitoring security events
* Detecting suspicious activities
* Investigating alerts
* Responding to incidents

SOC teams work **24/7** to protect organizations from cyber threats.

---

## 🎯 Objectives of a SOC

* Detect attacks as early as possible
* Minimize damage from incidents
* Protect sensitive data
* Ensure business continuity

---

## 🧩 Key SOC Functions

### 🔍 Monitoring & Detection

* Continuous monitoring of systems, networks, and users
* Identifying suspicious patterns and anomalies

---

### 🚨 Incident Response

* Investigating alerts
* Containing threats
* Recovering affected systems

---

### 🕵️ Threat Analysis

* Understanding attacker behavior
* Identifying Indicators of Compromise (IOCs)

---

### 📊 Log Analysis

* Analyzing logs from:

  * Servers
  * Firewalls
  * Applications
* Detecting unusual activities

## ⚠️ Challenges in SOC Work

* High number of alerts (alert fatigue)
* Distinguishing real threats from false positives
* Time pressure
* Evolving attack techniques

---

* Networking fundamentals

* Understanding attack techniques
* Critical thinking
* Attention to detail
