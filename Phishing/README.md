## 📌 Introduction

Think about it:

* How many of us have posted photos on social media today?
* How many of us have received a message saying *“Your account has been suspended, click here”*?

Almost all of us have experienced this.

Most cyberattacks are **not caused by complex hacking techniques**—they happen because attackers exploit human behavior using a technique called **Social Engineering**.

For example, if you scanned the QR code provided during the event and saw a message—this is a form of **Phishing**, which is one of the most common social engineering attacks.

---

## 🧠 What is Social Engineering?

**Social Engineering** is the art of manipulating people psychologically and emotionally to gain access to sensitive information or systems.

Instead of hacking systems, attackers **hack humans**.

---

## 🎣 What is Phishing?

**Phishing** is a type of social engineering attack where attackers try to trick users into giving sensitive information such as:

* Passwords
* Bank details
* Personal data

This is usually done through:

* Fake emails
* Fake websites
* Messages pretending to be from trusted entities (banks, companies, or government organizations)

The goal is to make the victim:

* Click a malicious link
* Download a harmful file
* Enter sensitive information

---

## ⚠️ Why Do Phishing Attacks Succeed?

Attackers rely on human psychology. The most common factors are:

### 1. Fear 😨

Example:
“You have suspicious activity on your account. Act now!”

---

### 2. Urgency ⏳

Example:
“Your account will be closed within 24 hours!”

---

### 3. Curiosity 🎁

Example:
“You won a prize! Click here to claim it.”

---

### 4. Trust 🤝

Example:
A fake call from “technical support” asking for your OTP or password.

---

## 🚨 Why is Social Engineering Dangerous?

These attacks can lead to:

* Account hijacking
* Financial theft
* Personal data leaks
* Company breaches
* Malware infections

Sometimes, a simple click can lead to a full system compromise.

---

## 🧩 Types of Phishing

1. **Email Phishing** – Fake emails
2. **SMS Phishing (Smishing)** – Fake text messages
3. **Phone Phishing (Vishing)** – Calls pretending to be official
4. **QR Phishing** – Malicious QR codes
5. **Spear Phishing** – Targeted attacks on specific individuals or employees

---

## 🌍 Real-World Example

Even large companies are not safe.

In one case, attackers targeted employees at major companies like Google and Facebook by sending **fake invoices** that appeared legitimate.

* Employees believed the invoices were real
* Payments were made to attackers
* Total losses reached **over $100 million**

This shows that phishing is not a technical weakness—it is **human manipulation**.

---

## 🛡️ How to Protect Yourself (Golden Rules)

1. **Do not click any link before verifying it**
2. **Check the website domain carefully**
3. **Never share passwords or OTP codes**
4. **Do not trust urgent or threatening messages**
5. **Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)**
6. **Use a password manager**
